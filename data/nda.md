# NON-DISCLOSURE AGREEMENT

**This Non-Disclosure Agreement** ("Agreement") is entered into as of the date last signed below ("Effective Date") between:

**Vertex Solutions Pte. Ltd.**, a company incorporated in Singapore with UEN [●] and having its registered office at [●], Singapore ("Disclosing Party"); and

**NovaTech Industries Pte. Ltd.**, a company incorporated in Singapore with UEN [●] and having its registered office at [●], Singapore ("Receiving Party").

The Disclosing Party and the Receiving Party are each referred to herein as a "Party" and collectively as the "Parties".

---

The Parties wish to explore a potential business collaboration and, in connection therewith, the Disclosing Party may disclose certain confidential information to the Receiving Party. The Parties desire to set out the terms on which such information will be disclosed, received, and protected.

**NOW, THEREFORE**, in consideration of the mutual covenants and agreements contained herein and other good and valuable consideration (the receipt and sufficiency of which are hereby acknowledged), the Parties agree as follows:

---

## 1. Definitions

**1.1** "Confidential Information" means any and all information or data, in whatever form or medium (whether oral, written, electronic, or otherwise), that relates to or concerns the Disclosing Party's business, operations, financial affairs, customers, suppliers, employees, contractors, technology, systems, processes, intellectual property, products, services, strategies, plans, forecasts, research, or any other matter pertaining to the Disclosing Party or its affiliates, subsidiaries, or associated entities, whether or not expressly designated as confidential at the time of disclosure.

**1.2** "Permitted Purpose" means the evaluation of a potential business collaboration between the Parties as described in any written proposal or term sheet exchanged by the Parties.

**1.3** "Representative" means, in relation to a Party, its directors, officers, employees, professional advisers, and contractors who have a need to know the Confidential Information for the Permitted Purpose and who are bound by confidentiality obligations no less stringent than those set out in this Agreement.

---

## 2. Disclosure and Obligations

**2.1** The Receiving Party shall hold all Confidential Information in strict confidence and shall not, without the prior written consent of the Disclosing Party, disclose any Confidential Information to any person other than its Representatives.

**2.2** The Receiving Party shall use the Confidential Information solely for the Permitted Purpose and for no other purpose whatsoever.

**2.3** The Receiving Party shall implement and maintain reasonable security measures to protect the Confidential Information from unauthorised disclosure or use, which measures shall be no less rigorous than those it applies to its own confidential information of similar sensitivity, and in any event no less than a reasonable standard of care.

**2.4** The Receiving Party shall promptly notify the Disclosing Party in writing upon becoming aware of any actual or suspected unauthorised disclosure or misuse of the Confidential Information.

---

## 3. Exceptions to Confidentiality

**3.1** The obligations of confidentiality under this Agreement shall not apply to any information that the Receiving Party can demonstrate, by clear and convincing evidence:

**(a)** was in the public domain at the time of disclosure or has subsequently entered the public domain through no act or omission of the Receiving Party;

**(b)** was lawfully in the possession of the Receiving Party prior to its disclosure by the Disclosing Party, without restriction on disclosure;

**(c)** was lawfully received by the Receiving Party from a third party who was not under any obligation of confidentiality with respect to such information; or

**(d)** is required to be disclosed by applicable law, regulation, or order of a court or regulatory authority of competent jurisdiction, provided that the Receiving Party gives the Disclosing Party prompt prior written notice of such requirement (to the extent permitted by law) and reasonably cooperates with the Disclosing Party in seeking a protective order or other appropriate relief.

**3.2** The Receiving Party may also claim that information was independently developed by it without reference to or use of the Confidential Information, but only where: (i) the Receiving Party maintained contemporaneous written records documenting the development process prior to and throughout the period of exposure to the Confidential Information, which records are sufficient to establish the independent development on a step-by-step basis; (ii) the Receiving Party, upon the Disclosing Party's reasonable request, makes such records available to an independent third-party auditor mutually agreed upon by the Parties and bears the full cost of such audit; and (iii) the Receiving Party gave prior written notice to the Disclosing Party at the time it commenced the relevant development activity that it intended to rely on this exception.

---

## 4. Term

**4.1** This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue in full force and effect for a period of **seven (7) years** from the Effective Date, unless earlier terminated by either Party on not less than thirty (30) days' prior written notice to the other Party.

**4.2** Termination or expiry of this Agreement shall not affect any rights or obligations that have accrued prior to the date of termination or expiry, including the Receiving Party's obligations with respect to Confidential Information disclosed prior to such date, which obligations shall survive termination or expiry of this Agreement.

---

## 5. Return or Destruction of Information

**5.1** Upon the written request of the Disclosing Party, or upon the termination or expiry of this Agreement, the Receiving Party shall promptly, at the Disclosing Party's election, either return or securely destroy all tangible materials containing or embodying Confidential Information (including all copies thereof), and shall certify such destruction or return in writing to the Disclosing Party.

**5.2** Notwithstanding the foregoing, the Receiving Party may retain one archival copy of Confidential Information solely to the extent required for compliance with applicable law or internal record-keeping policies, provided that such archival copy remains subject to the confidentiality obligations in this Agreement.

---

## 6. Intellectual Property

**6.1** Nothing in this Agreement shall be construed to grant the Receiving Party any right, title, interest, or licence in or to any Confidential Information or any intellectual property rights relating thereto.

**6.2** The Receiving Party acknowledges that all Confidential Information is and shall remain the exclusive property of the Disclosing Party (or its licensors, as applicable).

---

## 7. Remedies

**7.1** The Receiving Party acknowledges that any breach or threatened breach of this Agreement may cause irreparable harm to the Disclosing Party for which monetary damages would be an inadequate remedy, and that the Disclosing Party shall be entitled to seek equitable relief, including injunction and specific performance, without the requirement to post any bond or other security, in addition to all other remedies available at law or in equity.

**7.2** In addition to the remedies described above, and in recognition of the access the Receiving Party's personnel may have to the Disclosing Party's key employees and proprietary business knowledge through the disclosure process, the Receiving Party shall not, for a period of twenty-four (24) months from the date of termination or expiry of this Agreement, directly or indirectly solicit, recruit, induce, or attempt to solicit or recruit any employee of the Disclosing Party who was introduced to the Receiving Party or whose identity became known to the Receiving Party in connection with the Permitted Purpose, to leave the employ of the Disclosing Party.

**7.3** Nothing in this Agreement shall limit either Party's liability for fraud, wilful misconduct, or death or personal injury caused by negligence.

---

## 8. No Warranty

**8.1** The Disclosing Party makes no representation or warranty, express or implied, as to the accuracy, completeness, or fitness for any purpose of any Confidential Information disclosed pursuant to this Agreement. All Confidential Information is provided "as is".

**8.2** The Receiving Party acknowledges that it shall rely on Confidential Information at its own risk and that the Disclosing Party shall have no liability to the Receiving Party arising out of or in connection with the Receiving Party's use of, or reliance on, any Confidential Information.

---

## 9. Assignment

**9.1** Neither Party may assign, transfer, or otherwise dispose of any of its rights or obligations under this Agreement without the prior written consent of the other Party, which consent shall not be unreasonably withheld or delayed.

**9.2** Subject to clause 9.1, this Agreement shall be binding upon and inure to the benefit of the Parties and their respective successors and permitted assigns.

---

## 10. Notices

**10.1** Any notice required or permitted to be given under this Agreement shall be in writing and shall be delivered by hand, sent by registered post, or sent by email (with read receipt or delivery confirmation) to the address or email address of the relevant Party as set out in the signature block below, or to such other address or email address as a Party may notify to the other Party in writing from time to time.

**10.2** A notice sent by registered post shall be deemed to have been received three (3) Business Days after posting. A notice sent by email shall be deemed to have been received on the date of transmission, provided that the sender does not receive a non-delivery notification.

---

## 11. General

**11.1 Entire Agreement.** This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties with respect to its subject matter and supersedes all prior agreements, understandings, negotiations, and discussions, whether oral or written, between the Parties relating to the subject matter of this Agreement.

**11.2 Amendments.** This Agreement may not be amended, modified, or supplemented except by a written instrument duly executed by authorised representatives of both Parties.

**11.3 Waiver.** No failure or delay by either Party in exercising any right, power, or remedy under this Agreement shall operate as a waiver thereof, nor shall any single or partial exercise of any right, power, or remedy preclude any other or further exercise thereof or the exercise of any other right, power, or remedy.

**11.4 Severability.** If any provision of this Agreement is held by a court of competent jurisdiction to be invalid, illegal, or unenforceable, such provision shall be modified to the minimum extent necessary to make it valid, legal, and enforceable, and the validity, legality, and enforceability of the remaining provisions of this Agreement shall not in any way be affected or impaired thereby.

**11.5 Counterparts.** This Agreement may be executed in counterparts, each of which shall be deemed an original, and all of which together shall constitute one and the same instrument. Electronic signatures shall be deemed valid and binding to the same extent as original signatures.

---

## 12. Governing Law and Dispute Resolution

**12.1** This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of Singapore, without regard to its conflict of laws principles.

**12.2** Any dispute, controversy, or claim arising out of or in connection with this Agreement, or the breach, termination, or invalidity thereof, shall first be referred to mediation administered by the Singapore Mediation Centre in accordance with its prevailing rules. If the dispute is not resolved by mediation within thirty (30) days of the mediation request, either Party may refer the dispute to the courts of Singapore, which shall have exclusive jurisdiction to resolve such dispute.
